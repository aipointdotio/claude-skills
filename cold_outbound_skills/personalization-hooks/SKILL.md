# Personalization Hooks

Two hook types. Choose based on deal size and available research.

---

## Strong Hook — Direct Quote

Use when you have content to reference (post, podcast, article, interview).

```
In your [content type] about [topic], you mentioned "[direct quote]"...
```

Examples:
- `In your post about SDR burnout, you mentioned "the biggest issue is lack of quality leads"...`
- `On your podcast episode on scaling sales, you said "we struggled with pipeline predictability until"...`

**Use for:** enterprise/high-value accounts, competitive displacement, when you have strong quotable content.

---

## Lite Hook — Conceptual Tie

Use when content isn't quotable or volume is high.

```
I noticed you're focused on [theme]...
```

Examples:
- `Noticed you're scaling your SDR team...`
- `Saw you've been posting about RevOps challenges...`

**Use for:** mid-market volume, time-constrained personalisation, when a champion already exists inside the account.

---

## Which to Use

| Situation | Hook |
|---|---|
| Enterprise / $100K+ deal | Strong |
| Competitive displacement | Strong |
| Mid-market | Either |
| SMB / high-volume | Lite |
| Champion already inside | Lite |

---

## Full Opening Line Examples

**Strong Hook:**
```
In your post about outbound being "broken," you mentioned cold emails getting <1% reply rates.

We helped [similar company] hit 15%+ by switching to signal-based targeting.

Worth comparing notes?
```

**Lite Hook:**
```
Noticed you're scaling the BDR team at {{company}}.

Most BDR leaders I talk to are wrestling with ramp time and pipeline quality — we helped [similar company] cut ramp by 40%.

Is that on your radar?
```

---

## How to Run This Skill

1. Ask for the prospect's name, role, company, and any research (LinkedIn posts, articles, podcast clips, job postings).
2. Ask what the offer or value prop is (so the hook connects to something relevant).
3. Choose Strong or Lite based on the table above.
4. Write 1-3 hook options. Label each Strong or Lite.
5. For Strong Hooks: tie the quote directly to the offer's outcome. Don't just repeat what they said — connect it to a result.
6. For Lite Hooks: keep it one line. Don't over-explain the theme.
