# Viral LinkedIn Post System

Write LinkedIn posts using frameworks from accounts generating 500K+ impressions per month. The goal is posts that stop the scroll, deliver real value, and drive comments.

**Critical rule: never use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–) anywhere in a post.** Use a hyphen (-) or break into two sentences instead.

---

## Step 1: Pick Your Template

Ask the user what they want to post about, then choose the right template:

| Situation | Template to use |
|---|---|
| Showing a system with multiple agents/steps | Agent-Based System |
| Announcing a new tool, feature, or capability | New Feature/Tool |
| Introducing a trend or industry shift | Trend/Shift |
| Offering a free resource or list | Giveaway |
| Showcasing a single agent or automation | Single Hire |

If unclear, ask: "What's the main thing you want people to take away from this post?"

---

## Step 2: The Core Formula

Every post follows this structure:

```
HOOK (1-2 lines)
  Stop the scroll. Create tension, curiosity, or contrast.

CONTEXT (2-4 lines)
  Why this matters. The problem or shift.

BREAKDOWN (main body)
  The system, steps, or agents. Formatted for scanning.

PROOF (2-4 lines)
  Results, numbers, or logic that makes it credible.

CTA (3-4 lines)
  Single-word comment trigger + repost ask.
```

---

## Step 3: Hook Patterns

Use the hook pattern that fits the topic. Choose one.

### "I stopped X. Now I Y."
Creates contrast. Implies transformation.

- "I stopped cold outreach. Now I use 2 AI agents to find people who already want to hear from me."
- "I stopped hiring SDRs. Now I use 1 GTM Engineer + 20 AI agents."

**Use when:** Replacing an old approach with a new one.

---

### "I built [number] [things] that [result]."
Specific number + concrete outcome. Promises a system people can steal.

- "I built 7 AI agents that replace $300K in hires."
- "I get 50-100K impressions weekly. Because I built 7 AI agents that do the work for me."

**Use when:** Showing a complete system with multiple components.

---

### "You can literally [impressive thing] using [unexpected method]."
Challenges assumptions. Makes the impossible feel achievable.

- "You can literally build $5K+ agents using Claude Code on your phone."

**Use when:** A new tool or feature enables something surprising.

---

### "There's a new [thing] taking over [industry]."
Trend-based. Creates FOMO. Positions the author as ahead of the curve.

- "There's a new role taking over B2B sales. It's not an SDR. It's not an AE."

**Use when:** Introducing a new concept, role, or trend.

---

### "Just wanted to introduce my new hire."
Personification. Makes AI tangible and relatable.

- "Just wanted to introduce my new hire. It does all my prospect research before sales calls."

**Use when:** Showcasing a single agent or automation.

---

### "I collected [number] [things]. And I'm giving it away."
Generosity + specificity. High perceived value.

- "I collected 300 AI agents over the past year. And I'm giving away the full list — for free."

**Use when:** Offering a resource, list, or compilation.

## Step 4: Formatting Rules

**Line breaks are everything.**
- One thought per line.
- Never more than 2 sentences in a row.
- White space = readability on mobile.

**Bold section headers.**
Use Unicode bold text for agent names and section headers in the post body. Example: copy `𝗔𝗴𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝟭` style text from a Unicode bold converter.

**Arrow lists.**
Use `→` for features and steps, not bullet points.

**Contrast format.**
```
Not: "I saw you work at [Company]..."
But: "Your comment about CRM data being a mess hit home."
```

**Number your agents/steps.**
Agent 1, Agent 2, Agent 3 — or give them names: The Researcher, The Writer, The Coordinator.

---

## Step 5: Tone Rules

**Do:**
- Be direct. No hedging, no "I think" or "maybe."
- Use "you" — talk TO the reader.
- Short sentences. Punchy.
- Casual but confident. Like texting a smart friend.
- Use specific numbers when you have them.

**Never:**
- Use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–). Use hyphens (-) or periods instead.
- Use corporate buzzwords: leverage, synergy, ecosystem, empower.
- Use generic AI hype: revolutionary, cutting-edge, game-changing.
- Over-explain. Trust the reader.
- Use hashtags in the post body.
- Start with "In today's world" or "As we all know."
- Use more than one or two emojis (usually none).

---

## Step 6: CTA Formula

Always end with:

```
[Action verb] "[SINGLE WORD IN ALL CAPS]" and I'll send you [specific thing].

♻ Repost if your network needs this.
```

The CTA word should be:
- ALL CAPS
- Related to the topic (LEADS, CONTENT, TEAM, SYSTEM, PLAYBOOK)
- One word, easy to type

**Examples:**
- Drop "LEADS" and I'll send you the setup guide.
- Comment "SYSTEM" and I'll send it over.
- Comment "HIRED" and I'll send the full breakdown.

## Pre-Post Checklist

Before outputting the final post, verify:

- [ ] Hook fits in 1-2 lines (visible before "see more" on LinkedIn)
- [ ] Hook creates tension, curiosity, or contrast
- [ ] No em dashes (—) or en dashes (–) anywhere
- [ ] One thought per line throughout
- [ ] No corporate buzzwords or generic AI hype
- [ ] Specific numbers or examples where possible
- [ ] CTA uses a single ALL-CAPS word
- [ ] Repost line included
- [ ] No hashtags in the post body
- [ ] No fake stats or unverified claims
- [ ] Total length: 300-500 words

## What Makes a Post Go Viral

**Systems, not tips.**
Never "5 tips for better outreach."
Always "Here's the system I built" or "Here's exactly how the agents work."

**Specificity over hype.**
Not: "AI will transform sales"
But: "1.2 humans + 20 AI agents replaced 10 SDRs. $1M+ pipeline in 90 days."

**Give away the playbook.**
Don't tease — show the actual structure, the actual agents, the actual workflow. The CTA is for the detailed guide.

**Name things with personality.**
Not "agent that does research" but "The Researcher."
Not "content agent" but "The Brand Voice Gate."

**One strong insight per post.**
Every post has ONE clear takeaway. Everything else supports it.
