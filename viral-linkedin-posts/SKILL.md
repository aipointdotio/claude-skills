# Viral LinkedIn Post System

## Overview

This skill helps write LinkedIn posts using proven frameworks that have generated 700K+ impressions and 500+ comments per post in 40 days.

**Important:** This is not about copying exact words. It's about understanding the PATTERNS that work and applying them to new topics. You still need to provide real value. This skill helps you package that value so people actually see it.

## Hook Patterns That Work

### Pattern 1: "I stopped X. Now I Y."
Creates contrast. Implies transformation. Makes people curious what changed.

**Examples:**
- "I stopped cold outreach. Now I only use 2 AI agents to find people who WANT to hear from me."
- "I stopped hiring SDRs. Now I use 1 GTM Engineer + 20 AI agents."

**When to use:** When introducing a new approach that replaces an old one.

---

### Pattern 2: "I built/use [number] [things] that [impressive result]."
Specific number + concrete outcome. Promises a system people can steal.

**Examples:**
- "I built 7 AI agents that replace $300K+ in hires."
- "I get 50-100K impressions weekly on LinkedIn. Because I built 7 AI agents that do the work for me."

**When to use:** When showing a complete system with multiple components.

---

### Pattern 3: "You can literally [impressive thing] using [unexpected method]."
Challenges assumptions. Makes the impossible feel possible.

**Examples:**
- "You can literally build $5K+ agents using Claude Code on your phone."

**When to use:** When a new feature or tool enables something surprising.

---

### Pattern 4: "There's a new [thing] taking over [industry]."
Trend-based. Creates FOMO. Positions you as ahead of the curve.

**Examples:**
- "There's a new role taking over B2B sales. It's not an SDR. It's not an AE."

**When to use:** When introducing a new concept, role, or trend.

---

### Pattern 5: "Just wanted to introduce my new hire."
Personification. Makes AI feel tangible and relatable.

**Examples:**
- "Just wanted to introduce my new hire. It's an AI agent that does all the research before my sales calls."

**When to use:** When showcasing a single agent or automation.

---

### Pattern 6: "I collected/documented [number] [things]. And I'm giving it away."
Generosity + specificity. Huge perceived value.

**Examples:**
- "I collected 300 AI agents over the past year. And I'm giving away the full list - for free."

**When to use:** When offering a resource, list, or compilation.

## Tone Rules

### DO:
- Be direct. No hedging. No "I think" or "maybe."
- Use "you" - talk TO the reader
- Short sentences. Punchy.
- Casual but confident. Like texting a smart friend.
- Use specific numbers when you have them
- Say "Here's how" or "Let me break it down" - implies value coming

### DON'T:
- Use em dashes (â€”) or en dashes (â€“). Ever. Use hyphens (-) or periods instead.
- Use corporate buzzwords (leverage, synergy, ecosystem, empower)
- Use generic AI language (revolutionary, cutting-edge, game-changing)
- Over-explain. Trust the reader.
- Use emojis excessively (one or two max, usually none)
- Start with "In today's world" or "As we all know"
- Use hashtags in the main post body

## What Makes This System Different

### 1. Systems, Not Tips
Never "5 tips for better outreach."
Always "Here's the system I built" or "Here's how the agents work."

- Not: "AI will transform sales"

## Post Length Guidelines

- **Hook:** 1-2 lines (must be visible in the LinkedIn preview before "see more")
- **Context/Setup:** 3-5 lines
- **Main Breakdown:** 15-30 lines depending on complexity
- **Proof/Results:** 2-4 lines
- **CTA:** 3-4 lines

Total: ~300-500 words. Long enough to deliver value, short enough to not lose people.
